Problem solving

I have had many instances where I have been blocked on a problem or thought I had the problem right and didn’t quite understand why my code wasn’t passing. To problem solve I would get in touch with one of my friends in my cohort and talk through the problem with them. More often than not, as I was explaining it to them, I would see my error and be able to fix it myself or if I didn’t manage to figure it out after explaining it to them, they would then do their best to explain how they understood it in simpler terms which would often lead to me having a greater understanding and being able to work through the problem. Throughout the process I felt supported and it really helped me to learn about breaking a problem down into smaller, more manageable chunks so it wasn’t such a daunting task. During Sprint 4 I was dreading the Javascript Kata exercises. I don’t know why exactly, I guess I knew that my JavaScript skills are very limited and that it would mean I would be struggling my way through this long challenge. When I first opened the Variables & Objects exercise I was surprised that I felt confident in my ability to write the code that was needed. I wrote what I thought the answer was and found it didn’t pass the test. Immediate disappointment. After reading the code errors I found that I had spelt a name wrong so swiftly corrected it and passed the test! I felt that I must underestimate myself and learnt that I need to trust the process and that it will all come in time.
