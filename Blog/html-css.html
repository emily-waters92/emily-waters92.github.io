What is the difference between margin, border & padding?

Margins and padding are essentially blank space, and a border is just that – a border around an element! The difference between padding and margins is that padding controls how much whitespace or ‘breathing room’ is IN a box whereas a margin
controls how much whitespace there is OUT of a box, just like invisible barriers around it. CSS uses what is called ‘the box model’ to tell browsers how to display and space elements on a web page.

  • Content - The middle of the box. Content could be an image, text, heading etc
  • Padding - The space between the content and the border of your box.
  • Border - The perimeter of the box. Borders can be invisible or they could be a thick colored line like the green one pictured above.
  • Margin - The outer space surrounding the box.
We can use CSS padding to:
  • Adjust the whitespace directly around content
  • Adjust the size of a box or HTML elements
We can use CSS margin to:
  • Center boxes
  • Adjust whitespace surrounding boxes
  • Overlap elements
