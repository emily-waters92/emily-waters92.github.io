My learning plan

What are three new things you have learnt about yourself and your ego due to the core learning?
- I have learnt that I am used to grasping concepts quickly and when I don’t, I get frustrated.
- I have learnt that I can be very critical of myself.
- I have learnt that get distracted easily.

What is the role of values, empathy, and self-awareness in learning and programming?
Being self-aware of your learning style is important so that you can use it to your advantage, as well as knowing your limitations. Having strong values and empathy is important for anyone who is working for a company and/or in a team/collaboration and are traits that are valued by most employers.

What has surprised you the most about the core learning?
The fact that I have gone from a fixed mindset to a growth mindset in recent years without even knowing it! I loved the dive into neuroplasticity and the growth mindset and was surprised that even just knowing about neuroplasticity can help you change your brain make-up.

What were the most challenging aspects of the core learning?
I didn’t find any of the core learning challenging, I really enjoyed it and found it very insightful.

Why do you think we, a programming school, are spending so much time focusing on core learning in a web development Bootcamp course?
I think that it is an important part of the curriculum as it will set us apart from the other candidates when applying for jobs. It’s all very well to be able to code but there is so much more that goes into employer-employee and co-worker relationships.

Does the time you spent studying core learning here feel like a waste of time? Should you have just used that time to practise programming instead? Justify your answer.
No. Not only did I really enjoy it, I think it plays a vital role in everyday life as well as helping with employability. I also think understanding about the growth mindset helps with the initial shock of pace that I’m sure most of us students went through when first starting Foundations.

Have you noticed your strengths in practice?
Yes, in particular perspective and humour. Perspective has been helpful when it comes to solving coding challenges and humour when I am noticing I am starting to get frustrated.

Had any setbacks or challenges this week? What did you do to overcome these? What will you do differently next time? What was a highlight for you?
Yes, I have found coding from (basically) scratch quite difficult and challenging. To overcome this, I broke it down into steps and used pseudocode as well as reaching out to my peers for help. Next time I will probably do the same as I find that pseudocode (whilst seemingly difficult at first) is quite helpful and my peers are a great strength to me to keep me upbeat and remind me that I can do this. A highlight would have been going into campus and meeting a few people from my cohort for the first time. Loved that.

What is your long-term goal and/or career pathway?
I would still like to look at becoming a website designer but I think that eventually I would like to make my way into full stack development. Again, working from home would be great so I can still be involved in my daughters after school activities.

What do you think your biggest strengths and limitations will be in Bootcamp?
My strengths are my ability to keep going and not give up but I have found self-doubt creeping in over the course of Foundations which is not something I expected. I think this may be hinder me during Bootcamp as well as my ability to get distracted.

What do you think your biggest non-technical challenge at Bootcamp will be?
I think staying on top of the material may be the biggest challenge for me during Bootcamp. I am worried that my coding ability may not be fast enough and so it will slow me down and take me longer to get through the material.

What non-technical skills - human skills - would you like to see developed in yourself while at Dev Academy?
Improving my self-awareness and reflective tools.

What are your expectations from the Bootcamp team?
Support without judgement when I need it as well as patience and kindness.

What are your expectations of yourself on Bootcamp?
I will stop and take regular breaks so as not to get stressed out or burnt out. I will present the best of myself to facilitators, industry and community representatives as I myself am a representative of Dev Academy. I will change it up if I am feeling stuck or stressed – go for a walk outside, go and get a coffee, change up the scenery. I will put my best effort in everyday and give my all to this programme, my peers and my facilitators. I will ask for help when needed and continue to use Toggl Track so I know how much time I have spent on a particular project.
