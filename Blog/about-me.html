1. Explain a situation where you have made an ethical decision. Discuss how you weighed up the values involved in that decision, the decision you made, and how you reflect on the decision now.

An ethical decision that I have made was to adopt my dog instead of buying a ‘designer puppy’ from a breeder.
Whilst it would be nice to choose the colour, breed and gender of my dog, I consider breeders to be unethical in the sense that a lot choose to disregard the physical and emotional health of the breeding dog and that is not something that I could support.
As a compassionate, empathetic person I also consider breeders unnecessary. There are so many unwanted dogs out there that could end up being euthanised through no fault of their own, so why wouldn’t I adopt one of them instead?
In doing so, not only do I have the most loyal, loving and grateful dog ever, but I made an ethical decision and saved their life. Buying pets from breeders supports the breeder’s business and in doing so, encourages them to continue.
Little thought goes into the breeding animals’ emotional health, not to mention the physical aspect of all the pregnancies they have to endure – they are simply a means to making the breeder more money.
My adopted best friend is just that – my best friend. He really is the best dog I could have hoped for and I feel so happy that I have saved him from a life of being chained up, neglected and potentially abused.

2. Describe how your culture and the people around you have influenced your values and identity.

As a Kiwi, and having grown up surrounded by Kiwi’s in this big melting pot we call Aotearoa, I find that I have garnered the typical Kiwi identity of being a friendly, relaxed and welcoming person.
I am also not one to keel-over at the slightest inconvenience (Dad’s words of “Pull your socks up and get on with it!” still ring in my ears) and have the inventive, D-I-Y Kiwi spirit.
My parents (Dad-English, Mum-Kiwi) have absolutely influenced my values and identity with majority of my values aligning with theirs such as generosity and determination, however I have found that many relationships throughout my life have also added to these values.
Having been in a long-term relationship with someone who suffered severely from mental health problems, I have found that I have learned to be more empathetic and compassionate towards people I meet – you never know what people are going through!
I have also learned through the birth of my daughter, tolerance and equanimity - which is certainly not something my mother taught me!
